EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Membership

The studio ended December with 164 active members of 217 total, with 53 suspended. This represented a 15% decline in total membership from October's 256 and a 29% drop in active members from 232, driven by seasonal holiday patterns, operational challenges, and lack of formal retention strategies during the studio's first full quarter of operations since opening August 30, 2025. Early January indicators show recovery momentum with active members rebounding to 175 and suspensions improving to 30, suggesting the December trough represented temporary seasonal disruption.

Revenue

Q4 2025 generated $197,845 in total revenue, with membership revenue comprising 80.2% at $158,688, establishing a strong recurring revenue foundation. Package revenue contributed $24,083 (12.2%), spiking to $12,506 in November due to Black Friday promotions, while intro offers generated $7,452 (3.8%). Monthly revenue declined 20% from October's $71,386 to December's $56,243 as active membership contracted during the holiday period.

Utilisation

Session utilisation declined from 64-65% in October/November to 53.59% in December, falling below the optimal 70-75% target range. The 12 percentage point drop reflected the compounded impact of doubled suspensions (24 to 53 members), holiday travel patterns, and operational issues affecting member engagement. Average revenue per visit improved 2.4% from $23.52 to $24.08 across the quarter despite reduced class volume during the holiday period.

Sales vs Cancellations

December recorded 6 memberships sold against 30 cancelled members (12.88% attrition rate), representing the quarter's weakest performance. January data so far shows immediate recovery with 12 memberships sold, 16 cancellations, and attrition improving to 7.37%, alongside intro offers rebounding 154% to 89 units and first visits increasing 75% to 91.

Key Issues

  • Attrition exceeding target: December attrition spiked to 12.88%—nearly double the sub-7% target—with cancellations escalating from 16 to 30 across Q4 due to air conditioning issues, lack of formal retention strategies, and holiday travel patterns compounding operational challenges.
  • Lead generation volatility: First visits declined 48% from 100 in October to 52 in December during the holiday period, though January recovery to 91 visits indicates successful pipeline rebuilding through the $50 STRONG Starter promotion.
  • Membership conversion challenges: Sales collapsed 83% from 35 to 6 during Q4, driven by Black Friday package discount focus diverting conversions and operational issues undermining member experience during the critical onboarding window.
  • Utilisation below target: December's 53.59% utilisation fell significantly below the 70-75% optimal range as suspended memberships doubled to 53 and operational issues reduced member frequency and engagement.
  • Operational excellence gaps: Air conditioning centre issues and amenities problems prevented the studio from delivering the world-class experience required for retention during the vulnerable first-quarter period post-opening.

Active Interventions Being Deployed

  • Recovr retention software: Implemented this month with actioning now underway, providing systematic retention tracking and engagement protocols to proactively identify at-risk members and intervene before cancellations occur. Ongoing training continues to maximise team effectiveness and competency in using the platform.
  • Active pipeline management: Implemented this month with actioning now underway, rolling out structured lead nurturing processes with FOH team actively engaging prospects through the conversion funnel. Ongoing training continues to maximise effectiveness and competency in improving conversion rates.
  • Restructured FOH team with weekly schedules: Deploying defined weekly schedule framework covering acquisition and retention duties, ensuring consistent execution of member touchpoints and operational standards that were inconsistent during Q4.
  • $50 STRONG Starter promotion: Running 15-day unlimited intro offer until mid-February to drive top-of-funnel activity, already demonstrating success with January's 154% rebound in intro offers to 89 units.
  • Operational excellence initiatives: Addressing facility issues (air conditioning, amenities) with urgency to deliver the world-class studio experience required for member retention and satisfaction.
  • Team training and accountability: Building proficiency with operational framework between FOH and trainers, setting higher standards and expectations to ensure all leads are current and pipeline management becomes standard practice.

Expected Outcomes

  • Reduce attrition from 12.88% towards sub-7% target through activation of Recovr retention software, proactive member engagement protocols, and resolution of operational issues that drove December's elevated cancellations.
  • Rebuild membership conversion rates by transforming the January pipeline (89 intro offers, 91 first visits) into consistent monthly sales through improved FOH execution of the operational framework and systematic follow-up processes.
  • Optimise utilisation from December's 53.59% towards the 70-75% target range by reducing suspensions, improving member frequency through enhanced experience delivery, and leveraging available capacity without schedule expansion.
  • Execute consistent operational processes that support world-class member experience, driving retention through facility excellence (resolved air conditioning, functional amenities) and adherence to established standards across the team.

SUMMARY & PATH FORWARD

STRONG Pilates Castle Hill navigated a challenging Q4 2025 during its first three full months of operation since opening August 30, 2025. The studio generated $197,845 in revenue with 80.2% from recurring memberships, demonstrating a solid revenue foundation despite membership declining 15% from 256 to 217 and active members dropping 29% from 232 to 164 by December. While these declines appear significant, the fitness industry typically experiences pronounced seasonality during the holiday quarter, with a combination of suspensions and cancellations heavily skewing membership metrics during this period. The team has responded with urgency to reverse this trend, and January data indicates early positive trajectory with intro offers rebounding 154% to 89 units, first visits increasing 75% to 91, and attrition improving to 7.37%. The quarter revealed significant operational and retention challenges, with attrition spiking from 6.84% to 12.88% (nearly double the sub-7% target), suspensions doubling from 24 to 53 members, and utilisation falling from 65% to 54% in December. These challenges stemmed from air conditioning centre issues, no active retention strategies yet formally in place, holiday travel patterns, and membership sales collapsing 83% from 35 to 6 as Black Friday package promotions diverted conversions.

The path forward centres on urgent execution of known solutions already being implemented. The team must prioritise operational excellence by resolving facility issues (air conditioning, amenities) to deliver the world-class experience members expect, while simultaneously activating the newly deployed retention infrastructure including Recovr software, active pipeline management, and the restructured FOH team with defined weekly schedules covering acquisition and retention duties. With utilisation capacity available (current 54% vs 70-75% optimal target) and January's pipeline recovery demonstrating market demand, growth will come from retention execution and conversion optimisation rather than schedule expansion. The $50 STRONG Starter promotion running until mid-February provides top-of-funnel support, but success hinges on the team's ability to work together, adhere to the operational framework, and execute consistent retention processes that transform intro offers into memberships while reducing the elevated 12.88% attrition back towards the sub-7% target through improved member experience and engagement.

Key Takeaways

  • Q4 generated $197,845 with 80.2% recurring membership revenue, facing seasonal headwinds with 15% membership decline and 29% active member drop expected during holiday quarter
  • Team responding with urgency to operational issues (air conditioning, amenities) and implementing retention infrastructure to reverse Q4 trends
  • January recovery (89 intro offers, 91 first visits, 7.37% attrition) indicates successful reversal of December downturn with upward trajectory established
  • Success requires continued execution on operational excellence and activation of retention systems (Recovr, pipeline management, FOH framework) to drive attrition towards sub-7% target
