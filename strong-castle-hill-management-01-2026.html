EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Membership

202 total members with 180 active and 22 suspended as of January 2026. Active rate reached 89.1%, improving from 75.6% in December following the holiday season recovery. Net membership decline of 7 members occurred (20 sold vs 27 cancelled), though this represented improvement from December's net loss of 24 members.

Revenue

Quarterly gross revenue totalled $189,210 across November-January. Revenue declined 19.9% from $70,216 in November to $56,243 in December before recovering to $62,751 in January. Membership revenue contributed 77.8% of quarterly income ($147,180), with December impacted by reduced package sales and lower membership count.

Utilisation

Booking utilisation averaged 73.55% in January, within the 70-85% optimal target range, recovering from December's 62.41%. Attendance utilisation reached 64.66%, below the 70-75% healthy threshold but improving from December's 53.59%. The 10-percentage-point gap between booking and attendance rates indicates members are reserving spots but not consistently showing up.

Sales vs Cancellations

January delivered 20 membership sales against 27 cancellations, resulting in net negative growth of 7 members. Intro offers sold surged 277% from 35 in December to 132 in January driven by the STRONG Starter Pass promotion. First visits jumped from 52 to 132, mirroring the intro offer surge and indicating successful lead generation.

Key Issues

  • Attrition above target: Member attrition remained elevated at 12.88% (Dec) and 12.44% (Jan), well above the sub-7% target, with December cancellations spiking to 30 members and creating net membership losses across all three months.
  • Attendance utilisation below threshold: Attendance utilisation averaged 61.17% across the quarter, remaining below the healthy 70-75% threshold despite booking utilisation averaging 70.40%, indicating a 10-percentage-point gap where members book sessions but don't consistently attend.
  • Membership decline trajectory: Total membership declined 14.4% from 236 to 202 members over the quarter, with cancellations exceeding sales in all three months, requiring both improved retention and increased sales volume.
  • Seasonal volatility impact: December showed steep declines across key metrics—membership dropped to 217, attendance utilisation fell to 53.59%, and suspended members spiked 96% to 53—demonstrating vulnerability to seasonal patterns.
  • Recurring revenue base erosion: Declining membership count directly impacts the recurring revenue foundation, with monthly membership revenue declining from $53,713 in November to $46,634 in January despite stable per-member revenue.

Active Interventions Being Deployed

  • Cancellation analysis initiative: Active effort to understand high cancellation numbers through member feedback and data analysis to identify root causes and inform targeted retention strategies addressing the elevated attrition rates.
  • STRONG Starter Pass promotion: Successfully drove January intro offer sales to 132 units (277% increase), creating substantial lead generation and pipeline opportunity requiring systematic conversion processes.
  • New intro offer structure: Mid-February launch of 5-days-for-$50 offer, which testing has shown increases conversion rates, providing improved entry point for trial members.
  • STRONG Challenge initiative: Launching to drive class attendance, member engagement, and retention through gamified participation and accountability mechanisms.
  • Recovr retention software: Supporting systematic retention efforts and pipeline management to address elevated attrition rates through structured follow-up and engagement protocols.
  • FOH restructure implementation: New structure creating seamless customer journey with ongoing refinement and training to optimise sales pipeline efficiency and conversion processes.
  • Operational process training: Ongoing sales training and collaboration with FOH to ensure routines are built around daily scheduled tasks, proficiency with software, and consistent execution of sales pipelines and retention protocols.

Expected Outcomes

  • Reduce attrition from current 12.44% toward sub-7% target through systematic execution of Recovr retention protocols, STRONG Challenge engagement initiatives, and insights from cancellation analysis.
  • Convert January's 132 intro offer sales into sustained membership growth through improved conversion processes, new intro offer structure, and consistent FOH pipeline execution.
  • Improve attendance utilisation from 61.17% toward 70-75% healthy threshold by closing the booking-to-attendance gap through better member engagement and accountability systems.
  • Stabilise and reverse membership decline trajectory by balancing retention improvements with increased sales conversion, targeting net positive membership growth.
  • Optimise booking utilisation within the 70-85% target range whilst improving show-up consistency to maximise revenue per available session capacity.
  • Build operational consistency through systematic process execution across sales, retention, and engagement systems, establishing foundation for sustainable long-term growth.

SUMMARY & PATH FORWARD

STRONG Pilates Castle Hill generated $189,210 in quarterly revenue with membership revenue representing 77.8% of total income, though total membership declined 14.4% from 236 to 202 members across the three-month period. The studio demonstrated operational resilience through the holiday season, with booking utilisation averaging 70.40% within the optimal target range, though attendance utilisation at 61.17% remained below the healthy 70-75% threshold. The January surge in intro offer sales to 132 units—driven by the STRONG Starter Pass promotion—created substantial pipeline opportunity, whilst elevated attrition rates above 12% in December and January highlighted retention as the primary operational challenge requiring immediate intervention.

The path forward centres on converting January's lead generation success into sustained membership growth whilst addressing the gap between booking and attendance rates. Systematic execution of retention protocols through Recovr software, combined with the upcoming STRONG Challenge initiative to drive engagement, should support efforts to reduce attrition toward the sub-7% target. The FOH restructure's ongoing refinement and the mid-February launch of the new 5-days-for-$50 intro offer provide operational foundation for improved conversion rates. Success depends on consistent process execution across sales pipelines, retention systems, and member engagement initiatives, with focus on transforming trial members into committed long-term clients whilst improving show-up rates among existing members.

Key Takeaways

  • Quarterly revenue of $189,210 was anchored by stable membership revenue (77.8% of total), though declining member count from 236 to 202 presents growth challenges
  • January's intro offer surge to 132 units created significant pipeline opportunity requiring systematic conversion processes to realise membership growth
  • Attrition above 12% and attendance utilisation below 70% indicate retention and engagement as primary operational priorities
  • Upcoming initiatives (STRONG Challenge, new intro offer structure, Recovr retention systems) provide operational framework to address retention challenges and improve conversion rates
