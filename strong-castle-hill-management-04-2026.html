Executive Summary
Current State

Membership: Total membership contracted from 201 in February to 185 in April — a net loss of 16 members across the quarter. Active members fell from 184 to 161 while suspensions edged up from 17 to 24. The active rate held above 87% throughout, but the underlying base is shrinking and attrition has been the primary driver until April.

Revenue: Quarterly revenue totalled $163,744 — down from the prior quarter's $175,226 — with March the strongest at $58,062, followed by $54,429 in February and $51,253 in April. Membership revenue anchored the mix at 76.8%, 78.4%, and 81.3% of gross. April's softness reflects lower membership sales rather than base erosion across the recurring revenue stream.

Utilisation: Utilisation deteriorated across the quarter, with February the only month in target range. Booking moved from 77.42% through 63.48% to 58.04%, while attendance tracked at 68.29%, 56.28%, and 52.43%. The booking-to-attendance gap narrowed to 5.61% in April, indicating engagement quality among bookers is intact even as overall capacity goes underutilised.

Sales vs Cancellations: The quarter produced 67 membership sales against 74 cancellations — a net deficit of 7. Sales fell from 31 to 23 to 13 across the period, while cancellations dropped from 28 and 27 to 19. Attrition broke its multi-month pattern at 9.84% in April, the first sub-10% reading in five months.

Key Issues
  • Membership sales collapse: April produced just 13 memberships, the weakest reading in the rolling period and well below the funnel supply available.
  • Utilisation below target: April booking at 58.04% and attendance at 52.43% sit roughly 17 percentage points below optimal range.
  • Conversion gap: 66 intro offers and 75 first visits in April produced only 13 memberships — atypical for Castle Hill.
  • Net member deficit: 67 sold against 74 cancelled produced a net loss of 7, continuing the prior quarter's deficit pattern.
  • Attrition above target: April's 9.84% improved sharply but remains above the sub-7% benchmark, with sustained focus required.
  • First visits decline: Top-of-funnel volume fell from 107 in February to 75 in April, compressing acquisition supply.
Active Interventions Being Deployed
  • All-hands meeting 5 May: Full transparency session with FOH and trainers covering membership trajectory, customer journey ownership, and lifting in-class standards.
  • FOH and trainer cohesion protocol: Joint accountability on triallers, name use with every client, structured engagement at reception and in studio.
  • STRONG 25 Tracker full implementation: Now embedded as core process and culture, targeting the early-tenure window where most cancellations occur.
  • Trialler conversion discipline: Trainers advising next class booking before classes fill, FOH and trainers aligned on each trialler's stage and goals.
  • Acquisition Playbook emphasis: Continued reinforcement of phone and face-to-face tools within the Playbook to lift conversion from intro offer into membership.
  • Mother's Day and Breathwork events: Local collaborations with all sessions booked out, generating community visibility and acquisition opportunities through May.
Expected Outcomes
  • Lift conversion from intro offer into membership through tighter trialler engagement and FOH/trainer alignment on each prospect's journey.
  • Consolidate the April attrition improvement and continue movement from 9.84% toward the sub-7% target through STRONG 25 engagement.
  • Rebuild membership sales volume from April's 13 toward the prior quarter's 20–31 range as conversion discipline takes hold.
  • Improve booking utilisation from 58.04% toward the 75–85% optimal range through sustained engagement and acquisition recovery.
  • Restore positive net membership movement by sustaining the lower cancellation rate alongside recovered acquisition conversion through May and beyond.

Summary & Path Forward

Castle Hill closed the quarter with $163,744 in revenue, 67 memberships sold against 74 cancellations, and a net member deficit of 7. The defining shift of the period landed in April: attrition fell to 9.84% — the first sub-10% reading in five months, breaking a persistent 12%+ pattern — while membership sales dropped to 13, the weakest of the visible period. Utilisation contracted to 58.04% booking and 52.43% attendance, both well below target.

The binding constraint has shifted from retention to acquisition conversion. With 66 intro offers and 75 first visits in April producing only 13 memberships, the funnel has supply but is not closing. The all-hands meeting on Tuesday 5 May addresses this directly, realigning FOH and trainers on customer journey ownership, trialler engagement discipline, name use, social media presence, and lifting in-class standards. STRONG 25 Tracker is now in full cultural implementation to consolidate the retention gains.

Key Takeaways
  • Quarterly revenue $163,744 with net member deficit of 7 — sales 67, cancellations 74.
  • April attrition broke the 12%+ pattern at 9.84% — retention initiatives showing impact.
  • Acquisition conversion now the binding constraint — 66 intro offers and 75 first visits produced only 13 memberships.
  • All-hands meeting 5 May targets FOH/trainer cohesion, trialler discipline, and lifting in-class standards.
